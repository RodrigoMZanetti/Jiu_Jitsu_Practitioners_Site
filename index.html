The 6 Best Supplements for Jiu-Jitsu Practitioners

Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu isn’t just a sport. It’s a lifestyle built on technique, timing, and total commitment. But what separates good from great
often comes down to one factor: recovery.

You can train hard, but if your body isn’t fueled and recovering properly, you’ll hit a wall fast. That’s where supplements come in.

Today, we’re breaking down the six best supplements every serious BJJ practitioner should consider. This isn’t about hype or bro-science. It’s about smart, evidence-based choices to keep your body sharp, joints healthy, and performance consistent.

1. Creatine Monohydrate

If you train BJJ and you’re not taking creatine, you’re leaving gains on the table. Period.

Creatine helps your muscles produce energy during high-intensity training. It boosts your phosphocreatine stores in muscle tissue, which is used to generate ATP — the energy currency of your cells.

BJJ is full of explosive moments — bridging, passing, escaping. Creatine increases your short-burst power. You’ll notice stronger takedowns, quicker scrambles, and less fatigue But it’s not just about strength. Studies show creatine also helps with brain performance, including reaction time and memory — both huge in Jiu-Jitsu, where mental fatigue is real.

Research shows creatine improves strength, muscular endurance, and lean mass. Even better? It's safe. Decades of studies show no serious health risks in healthy individuals.

Dosage & Timing
  • Start with a loading phase: 20 grams/day for 5–7 days.
  • Then shift to maintenance: 3–5 grams/day.
  • Timing? Not crucial — just be consistent.
